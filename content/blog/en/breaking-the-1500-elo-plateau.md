**Breaking through a 1500 Elo plateau isn't about playing more — it's about working on the right weakness.** Stagnation almost always comes from two or three recurring mistakes that cost you whole games. Identifying and fixing them, one at a time, unlocks more points than hundreds of extra blitz games.

## What is a chess plateau?

A plateau is a period — often several months — where your rating stops moving despite steady play. It's not a lack of talent: it's the sign that **the tools that got you here are no longer enough for the next step up**.

## Why you stall at 1500 Elo

Three causes come back almost every time:

1. **Tactical blunders.** At this level, one game in two is still decided by a hung piece or an opponent's move you didn't see.
2. **Playing without a plan.** You string together isolated "good moves" with no guiding idea, and the position falls apart in the middlegame.
3. **Botched endgames.** Winning or drawn positions turn into losses for lack of basic technique.

## The method to start climbing again

You don't fix everything at once. You move in short cycles:

1. **Diagnose.** Analyse your last three rated losses, without an engine at first. For each game, note _the_ moment when things tipped and why. A pattern of error will emerge — that's your target.
2. **Target a single weakness** for 1 to 3 weeks (calculation, a poorly understood opening structure, an endgame). Not two.
3. **Work between games**, not only by playing: targeted exercises on the current weakness.
4. **Measure**, then move on to the next weakness.

The rule that changes everything: **consistency and targeting beat volume.** Two games analysed thoroughly are worth more than ten skimmed.

## How long does it take?

With targeted, regular work, most committed players see the plateau start to crack in **2 to 3 months**. The pace depends on your starting point and the time spent working between sessions — not on the number of games played.

## Putting it into practice

A plateau is cleared much faster with an outside eye that points to _the_ weakness to work on. That's exactly what coaching is for: a diagnosis of your games, a tailored plan, follow-up.
